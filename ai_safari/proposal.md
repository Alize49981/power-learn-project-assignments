 # Simple Proposal: SMS-Based Maternal Health Assistant

## 1. Introduction

We appreciate the important work your organization is doing to support pregnant women in rural communities. We understand that distance from health facilities, limited follow-ups, and access to timely information remain key challenges for many mothers.

---

## 2. The Problem

Many pregnant women in rural areas:

* Miss clinic appointments due to distance or lack of reminders
* Lack consistent follow-up after visits
* Have limited access to reliable health information
* May not recognize early danger signs

These gaps can lead to preventable complications for both mother and child.

---

## 3. Proposed Solution

We propose a **simple SMS-based maternal health assistant** designed specifically for rural settings.

The system works on **basic mobile phones (no internet required)** and supports mothers throughout pregnancy by sending:

* Appointment reminders
* Weekly health tips using familiar local foods (e.g., ugali, sukuma wiki)
* Alerts about important danger signs

---

## 4. How It Works

1. Pregnant women are registered using their phone numbers
2. They receive automated SMS messages based on their stage of pregnancy
3. Messages are sent in simple, clear language
4. The system runs continuously with minimal supervision

---

## 5. Benefits to Your Organization

* Improves attendance for clinic visits
* Strengthens follow-up without increasing staff workload
* Provides consistent, culturally relevant health education
* Reaches even remote mothers with basic phones
* Supports early detection of complications

---

## 6. Simple Pricing Idea

We suggest a **low-cost pilot program**:

* Small monthly fee or cost per enrolled mother
* Flexible based on program size
* Option to start with a limited group before scaling

---

## 7. Closing

This solution is designed to **complement your existing programs**, not replace them—helping extend care beyond clinic visits and into everyday life.

We would be glad to explore how this could fit into your current work.
